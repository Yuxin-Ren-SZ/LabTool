RT-qPCR Workflow Assistant

Browser-only planning and relative-expression helper for 96-well RT-qPCR setup. No data leaves this browser.

Designed for setup planning, reagent scaling, concentration calculations, and exploratory ΔΔCt analysis. Confirm final protocols and interpretation with your lab standard operating procedure.

Scope

What this tool does and does not do

This tool is a static LabTools page for RT-qPCR setup planning and relative-expression calculations. It accepts pasted or uploaded CSV text, produces browser-side tables, and exports CSV files for manual review.

In scope
  • 96-well plate planning with customizable layout mode and technical replicates.
  • Primer master mix and sample cDNA/H2O mix scaling with explicit overage.
  • Generic nucleic acid concentration calculations from Nanodrop, Qubit, or similar CSV-style output.
  • Single- or multi-reference ΔΔCt analysis with configurable control treatment and PCR efficiency.
  • CSV export of generated plate maps, reagent tables, concentration tables, and ΔΔCt results.
Out of scope
  • No external dependencies, server upload, instrument-specific binary parsing, or permanent storage.
  • No diagnostic interpretation or statistical claims beyond summary means shown in the browser.
  • No automatic validation against a specific primer set, kit, or thermocycler protocol.
  • No guaranteed replacement for manual plate review before pipetting.
Import formats

Samples and assays can be entered as comma-separated lists. Measurement and Cq data use CSV rows with flexible headers. Export buttons only download generated CSV text; they do not alter checked-in config files.

Setup

Experiment inputs

Enter real experiment inputs here. The page starts blank by design so test data does not leak into exported plans.

Recommended sample name format is `CellLine_Treatment`. You can also provide explicit `CellLineName` and `Treatment` columns in Cq imports.

CSV assay rows may include `gene`, `assay_type` (`reference` or `target`), and `efficiency`. If omitted, genes listed under reference genes are treated as references.

One or more genes. Multiple references use the mean reference Cq per sample for ΔCt.

Paste or upload CSV from Nanodrop, Qubit, plate readers, or manual entry. Concentration headers are normalized when possible.

ΔΔCt

Relative expression analysis

Paste tidy Cq rows. The tool calculates ΔCt, ΔΔCt, fold change, a results table, and a compact ΔΔCt visualization.

Recognized headers include `sample_name`, `sample`, `Sample`, `assay/gene`, `target`, `Target`, `gene`, `Cq`, `Ct`, `CellLineName`, and `Treatment`.

Use 2.0 for 100% efficiency. Fold change uses efficiency ^ -ΔΔCt.

PCR efficiency here means amplification efficiency in the ΔΔCt model, not reagent overage. For example, 2.0 means product doubles every cycle; 1.9 means lower amplification efficiency.
